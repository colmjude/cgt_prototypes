GDS

We are leading the digital transformation of government, making public services digital by default, and simpler, clearer and faster to use.

Read more about what we do

The role

You’ll deliver high quality code, thrive using agile methods and work as part of a multidisciplinary team.

You must be familiar with modern frameworks and languages, as well as working practices such as TDD, continuous integration, continuous delivery and DevOps.

Main responsibilities

You’ll:

  • build web and mobile products to serve citizen and government needs
  • take the lead for technology on small projects
  • implement toolkits and APIs for integration, performance optimisation and security and scalability
  • build automated tests to support continuous deployment environment
  • act as a digital ambassador across government and the web community (eg posting blogs, tech talks at conferences)
  • participate in our in-house and out-of-hours support rota
  • help recruit developers

Skills and experience

Essential:

  • front-end and/or back-end experience, with detailed knowledge of at least one of the following: Ruby, Java, Python, Javascript, Scala or Go
  • CSS, HTML and Javascript skills (for front-end developers)
  • understanding of software design principles, problem solving and the ability to diagnose and debug production issues
  • experience of using testing to validate solutions
  • ability to research and learn new programming tools and techniques
  • understanding of agile environments, version control and technical debt
  • knowledge of technologies used for web applications, (eg databases, backups, CDNs and search) and of Unix-like operating systems (eg Linux and/or Mac OS)
  • experience of using third-party libraries and configuration management tools
  • understanding of web security practices (eg monitoring and intrusion detection)

Desirable

  • passion for digital services and agile working
  • ability to communicate technical concepts
  • experience of building and scaling high-traffic websites
  • history of leading technical projects

Civil Service Competencies

The successful applicant must show the following competencies from the Civil Service Competency Framework:

  • changing and improving
  • managing a quality service
  • collaborating and partnering
